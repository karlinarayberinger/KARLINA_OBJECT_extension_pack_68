Is it possible to drink so much green tea that I turn green?

ChatGPT:

No. Drinking green tea will not turn your skin green.

The green color in tea comes mostly from plant pigments and polyphenols, but your digestive system breaks them down or excretes them. They do not get deposited in your skin like a dye.

The realistic “too much green tea” concerns are different:

* **Too much caffeine**: jitteriness, anxiety, insomnia, fast heartbeat, nausea.
* **Too many tannins**: stomach upset or reduced iron absorption, especially if you drink it with meals.
* **Very concentrated green tea extracts/supplements**: rarely associated with liver injury. Brewed tea is much safer than concentrated extract.
* **Excess fluid intake**: in extreme cases, drinking huge volumes of any fluid can disrupt electrolytes, though that would require a lot.

So: you might feel wired, nauseated, or need to pee constantly before you ever turn even slightly green.

Can I overdose on green tea?

ChatGPT:

Yes, but **with normal brewed green tea it is hard to “overdose” in the dramatic poisoning sense** unless you drink extreme amounts. The more realistic problems are **caffeine overload, nausea/stomach irritation, insomnia/anxiety, reduced iron absorption, and — mainly with concentrated green-tea extract — rare liver injury**.

For most adults, the FDA cites **400 mg caffeine per day** as an amount “not generally associated with negative effects,” though sensitivity varies. Green tea is often around **~20–45 mg caffeine per 8 oz cup**, depending on type and steeping, so caffeine trouble is possible if you drink many cups, especially fast or on an empty stomach. ([U.S. Food and Drug Administration][1])

**Brewed green tea:** moderate intake is generally considered safe. The NIH/NCCIH notes that green tea as a beverage is believed safe in moderate amounts. ([NCCIH][2])

**Green-tea extract pills/powders:** much riskier. Concentrated extracts have been linked to a small number of liver-problem cases, and LiverTox notes that green tea extract, and more rarely very large amounts of brewed green tea, has been implicated in acute liver injury. ([NCBI][3])

Signs you’ve had too much caffeine/green tea can include **jitters, anxiety, nausea, diarrhea, headache, insomnia, fast heartbeat, tremor, dizziness, or vomiting**. More serious red flags include **chest pain, fainting, confusion, severe vomiting, irregular heartbeat, seizure, yellow skin/eyes, dark urine, or severe abdominal pain**. For those, seek urgent medical help.

A practical ceiling: **1–5 cups/day of brewed green tea is usually ordinary** for many adults. **8–10+ cups/day** may start getting excessive depending on caffeine sensitivity, iron status, medications, and sleep. I would be much more cautious with **extracts** than tea.
